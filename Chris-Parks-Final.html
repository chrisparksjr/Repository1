Pickling

Once upon a time, humans pickled to make less perishable. Now, we can pickle purely for the culinary satisfaction..

Cucumbers

According to televsion show Portlandia, you can pickle almost everything. In this instance, we focus on a more traditional approach: the cucumber. The Kirby variety has a thicker skin that can stand up to the pickling liquid, and remains crunchy, with a good snap, long after it's been preserved.















Spicing


Cucumbers arent typically regarded as a flavorful vegetable, so the introduction of spices is essentially. Pictured below are common spices used in a standard recipe, however, feel free to alter combinations to achieve more unique flavors.

Brining

Brining is the introduction of a sodium solution to the cucumber in this case, in order to neutralize bacteria through osmosis. From a more culinary approach, this is what provides the foundarion for the pickle.Prepare a brine using the ratio of two tablespoons of
salt to one quart of water. Stir well and set aside.



Canning

Remove air bubbles. We've covered everything going in the can, now lets get the can and fill it up. Using a glass mason jar with a ring and lid with intact seal, fill 3/4 full with cucumbers halved longways. Put spices in next, followed by brine being filled to the top. Seal the mason jar, and place in refridgerator. After 48 hours, open and enjoy!
